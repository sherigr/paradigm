Justin B. Gornell, PT, DPT, OCS

Justin B. Gornell  graduated from the College of the Holy Cross in 1997 with a Bachelor of Arts in Psychology and a concentration in Biological-Psychology. He graduated from New York University in 2001 as a Doctor of Physical Therapy. Dr. Gornell became board certified in orthopedics (OCS - orthopedic clinical specialist) in 2005.


Justin had been working with orthopedic and sports-related injuries in a typical out-patient physical therapy clinic for many years. That environment requires the therapist to treat multiple patients per hour and therefore never giving undivided attention for more than15 to 20 minutes. Justin believed the patients deserved better care. In 2009, he founded Paradigm Physical Therapy, to provide optimum care and treatment. Since there is no auxiliary staff at Paradigm, the patient is with the physical therapist during the entire session.


Dr. Gornell assists New York University's Physical Therapy program as a master clinician, teaching and educating students about orthopedic physical therapy conditions. He is an active member of the American Physical Therapy Association (APTA) and of their Orthopedic Section, constantly keeping up with current research.


Justin enjoys running, working out, skiing, as well as spending time with his family. He coaches and is very active with his four young boys.
