PHYSICAL THERAPY

Paradigm Physical Therapybegins the rehabilitation process with a thorough evaluation for each patient. An individual treatment program will be developed based upon the patients' goals and findings based on the initial evaluation. Patients will be educated abot their diagnosis and how their treatment program will address their deficits and prevent any future complications. The patient will have the anatomy/physiology, pathology and biomechanics of the affected body part explained so that the injury is properly understood. Treatment sessions will include therapeutic and functional exercises, manual therapy, postural education, gait training, stretches and modalities, when necessary. Prior to discharge, a home exercise program will be developed to achieve and maintain maximum function.

MANUAL THERAPY

A clinical approach utilizing skilled, specific hands-on techniques used to diagnose and treat soft tissues and joint structures for the purpose of modulating pain; increasing range of motion (ROM); reducing or eliminating soft tissue inflammation; inducing relaxation; improving contractile and non-contractile tissue repair, extensibility and/or stability; facilitating movement; and improving function. These therapeutic procedures inactive trigger points, warms muscles and encourages nutrient rich blood flow. It relieves pain, increases range of motion, promotes flexibility and accelerates recovery.
